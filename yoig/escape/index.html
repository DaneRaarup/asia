Escape from North Korea

March 14th, 2017

I watched videos about the experiences of people who have escaped from North Korea and my questions and comments on the videos are below.

I was absent for the first video, so my questions and comments are not posted here.

Hyeonseo Lee

March 14th, 2017

The video I watched was titled "Escape from North Korea" and it was a TED talk.

My questions and comments are as follows:

  • How was that the first experience when she had heard of poverty in her country was when she was seven years old?
  • Why did the 1990s famine in North Korea hit? What caused it? Was it directly related to the new lack of support from the Soviet Union?
  • There is so much death, from famine and attempted escape mentioned in the video.

Zeina

March 16th, 2017

This video, from Mashable Docs, is actually about escaping from Syria.

My questions and comments are as follows:

  • I am surprised that civilians are in danger as much as they are in Syria.
  • Is there no "normal" person in Syria whose home or workplace or school is not targeted for bombings?
  • I find it impressive that they were able to escape and now live a life that is normal for the United States.

Mohammed and Shofar

March 16th, 2017

These people have escaped from Burma (now Myanmar), and tell their story in this video by the Globe and Mail.

My questions and comments are as follows:

  • Was there a reason other than racial segregation that the Burmese government prohibited these students from attending school?
  • What was life like in Burma prior to the escape?
  • It's crazy how much they have improved in their schoolwork from the teacher's description.
  • Why are they hated and targeted by the government of Burma?
  • I find it sad that the vast majority of the people who escaped on the boat that Mohammed was on died.
